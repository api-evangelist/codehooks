<p>Every modern SaaS needs to react to events: a user signs up, a payment succeeds, a trial expires. Webhooks are the glue that connects these events to your business logic—but building reliable webhook infrastructure means managing servers, queues, retries, and databases.</p>
<p>Email automation platforms promise to handle this, but they come with baggage: vendor lock-in, limited webhook integrations, and opaque pricing that scales against you.</p>
<p>What if your webhook endpoints could directly power your email automation?</p>
<h2 class="anchor anchorWithStickyNavbar_LWe7" id="the-problem-with-email-automation-saas">The Problem with Email Automation SaaS<a class="hash-link" href="https://codehooks.io/blog/2026/01/01/webhook-email-automation#the-problem-with-email-automation-saas" title="Direct link to The Problem with Email Automation SaaS">​</a></h2>
<p>Mailchimp, ConvertKit, ActiveCampaign—they all solve the same problem: sending automated email sequences. But they come with baggage:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Limited webhook support</strong>: Want to trigger emails from Stripe events? Custom webhooks from your app? Good luck integrating.</li>
<li><strong>Vendor lock-in</strong>: Your subscribers live in their database</li>
<li><strong>Black boxes</strong>: Webhook failed? Email bounced? You'll never know why.</li>
<li><strong>Scaling costs</strong>: $50/month for 500 subscribers, $300/month for 10,000</li>
</ul>
<p>What if you could own the entire stack—starting with your webhook endpoints?</p>
<h2 class="anchor anchorWithStickyNavbar_LWe7" id="enter-codehooksio-webhooks-made-simple">Enter Codehooks.io: Webhooks Made Simple<a class="hash-link" href="https://codehooks.io/blog/2026/01/01/webhook-email-automation#enter-codehooksio-webhooks-made-simple" title="Direct link to Enter Codehooks.io: Webhooks Made Simple">​</a></h2>

<p>✅ <strong>Webhook-first architecture</strong> - Receive events from Stripe, signup forms, Zapier, or any service</p>

<h2 class="anchor anchorWithStickyNavbar_LWe7" id="why-codehooks-for-webhooks">Why Codehooks for Webhooks?<a class="hash-link" href="https://codehooks.io/blog/2026/01/01/webhook-email-automation#why-codehooks-for-webhooks" title="Direct link to Why Codehooks for Webhooks?">​</a></h2>
<p>Building reliable webhook infrastructure traditionally requires stitching together:</p>
<ul>
<li>Serverless functions (AWS Lambda)</li>
<li>API Gateway for HTTPS endpoints</li>
<li>Database (MongoDB Atlas)</li>
<li>Cron scheduler (CloudWatch Events)</li>
<li>Queue system (SQS) for async processing</li>
<li>Retry logic and dead letter queues</li>
<li>Environment secrets management</li>
</ul>
<p>That's a lot of DevOps for receiving a webhook.</p>
<p>Codehooks gives you all of this in <strong>one platform</strong>, purpose-built for webhooks:</p>
