<p>OpenClaw is everywhere right now. People are running "Jarvis" on Mac minis 24/7, automating their entire digital lives.</p>
<p>But here's the thing: OpenClaw runs locally. That's great for privacy and control. It's less great when you need:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>REST APIs and webhooks</strong> that stay up even when your computer sleeps</li>
<li><strong>Persistent storage</strong> beyond local files</li>
<li><strong>Background jobs</strong> that run on a schedule</li>
<li><strong>CRUD endpoints</strong> your other apps can talk to</li>
</ul>
<p>You <em>could</em> give your agent access to AWS. Let it wire up Lambda, API Gateway, DynamoDB, and SQS. Watch it burn through your free tier and leak credentials.</p>
<p>Or you could give it a backend designed for agents.</p>
<p><img alt="Codehooks backend for OpenClaw agents" class="img_ev3q" height="1024" src="https://codehooks.io/assets/images/codehooks-openclaw-17de5361beb4270c2ed9585e549c343e.webp" width="1536" /></p>

<p>Codehooks.io is a serverless backend with everything wired together: REST APIs, database, queues, cron, workers. Your agent can build anything from a simple CRUD API to a multi-step workflow — and deploy it in seconds.</p>

<h2 class="anchor anchorWithStickyNavbar_LWe7" id="why-this-matters-for-openclaw">Why this matters for OpenClaw<a class="hash-link" href="https://codehooks.io/blog/openclaw-backend#why-this-matters-for-openclaw" title="Direct link to Why this matters for OpenClaw">​</a></h2>
<h3 class="anchor anchorWithStickyNavbar_LWe7" id="1-apis-and-webhooks-that-dont-depend-on-your-device">1. APIs and webhooks that don't depend on your device<a class="hash-link" href="https://codehooks.io/blog/openclaw-backend#1-apis-and-webhooks-that-dont-depend-on-your-device" title="Direct link to 1. APIs and webhooks that don't depend on your device">​</a></h3>
<p>Your OpenClaw agent runs on your Mac mini. But what happens when Stripe sends a payment webhook at 3am and your machine is updating macOS? Missed event. What about the CRUD API your mobile app depends on? Down.</p>
<p>With Codehooks, your endpoints live in the cloud. Your agent deploys them, and they stay up whether your Mac is awake or not.</p>
<h3 class="anchor anchorWithStickyNavbar_LWe7" id="2-sandboxed-integrations">2. Sandboxed integrations<a class="hash-link" href="https://codehooks.io/blog/openclaw-backend#2-sandboxed-integrations" title="Direct link to 2. Sandboxed integrations">​</a></h3>
<p>Security researchers have called OpenClaw "a security nightmare" — and they're not wrong. An agent with full system access is powerful but risky.</p>
<p>Offloading sensitive integrations (payment webhooks, API keys, customer data) to a separate backend limits the blast radius. Your agent interacts with the backend via API, not raw credentials scattered across your machine.</p>

<h3 class="anchor anchorWithStickyNavbar_LWe7" id="4-fast-enough-for-the-agent-loop">4. Fast enough for the agent loop<a class="hash-link" href="https://codehooks.io/blog/openclaw-backend#4-fast-enough-for-the-agent-loop" title="Direct link to 4. Fast enough for the agent loop">​</a></h3>
<p>Deploys take seconds, not minutes. Your agent can:</p>
<ol>
<li>Run <code>coho prompt</code> to load context</li>
<li>Write code</li>
<li>Deploy with <code>coho deploy</code></li>
<li>Check logs with <code>coho log -f</code></li>
<li>Iterate</li>
</ol>
<p>Something break? Your agent reads the logs, fixes the code, and redeploys — all in the same loop. No rollback ceremony, no CloudFormation stacks. Just fix and ship.</p>
<h2 class="anchor anchorWithStickyNavbar_LWe7" id="get-your-agent-set-up-in-2-minutes">Get your agent set up in 2 minutes<a class="hash-link" href="https://codehooks.io/blog/openclaw-backend#get-your-agent-set-up-in-2-minutes" title="Direct link to Get your agent set up in 2 minutes">​</a></h2>
